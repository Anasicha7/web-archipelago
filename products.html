Processing Our Coffee.

Semi Wash

Semi-washed coffee processing is also known as the 'wet-hulled' process. The outer skin is removed, similar to washed coffee, but the mucilage - inner-mesocarp - remains on the parchment and is sun-dried. Then the parchment is removed. The swollen white beans enter the second drying-phase.

Full Wash

In the wet process, the coffee beans that have been picked go into the separation process (sorting). At this stage, the coffee beans are submerged in the water and if the beans float, this indicates that the beans are defective. These defective coffee beans are then separated from other coffee beans.

Natural

The natural process is a coffee processing process to separate the oldest coffee beans. As the name implies, this process does not involve machines or water. Coffee cherries that have passed the selection process according to their quality will go through a drying process or drying in direct sunlight.

Wine

The process of processing coffee wine starts with seeds that are not peeled and then dried and fermented three times to complete. The next process is peeling the coffee skin with a coffee grinder, to get the coffee and then drying it again.

Honey

In the honey process, the coffee cherries are mechanically peeled, but this method uses less water than the pulped natural process. The depth per machine will be controlled to determine how much flesh will remain attached to the seeds before drying.

Luwak

Kopi luwak is made from coffee beans plucked from civets' feces. This is bad news for civets. It's the world's most expensive coffee, and it's made from poop. Or rather, it's made from coffee beans that are partially digested and then pooped out by the civet, a catlike creature.
